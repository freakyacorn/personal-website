Wesley Oliver

PhD Candidate, Chemical & Biological Engineering

I'm a fourth year graduate student in the Chemical and Biological Engineering department at Princeton University. In my undergrad, I studied chemical engineering at Purdue University. My research focuses on using molecular simulation and machine learning to accelerate polymer materials design.

Research Projects

Physically-Informed Machine Learning for Protein Properties

In this work, we have explored the utiliy of "physically-informed" features that can improve machine learning models' ability to predict intrinsically disordered protein and polymer phase behavior from molecular simulation data.

scikit-learn LAMMPS Phase Separation Coarse-grained MD

Reinforcement Learning Control of Materials Morphology

This project is focused on using reinforcement learning (Q-learning) to control molecular simulations and influence the morphology and structure formation of sequence-defined materials.

Q-Learning PyTorch Enhanced Sampling

Atomistic MD & Free Energy Calculations

This work explores the structural impact of the incorporation of non-native nucleic acids into canonical DNA. Advanced computational methods including thermodynamic integration and free energy perturbation allow for characterization of structural stability.

GROMACS Free-energy Calculations (FEP/TI) Atomistic MD Dimensionality Reduction
